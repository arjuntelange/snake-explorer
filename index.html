Explore Venomous Snake Species

Learn about dangerous snakes from around the world

Eastern green mamba

The Eastern green mamba (Dendroaspis angusticeps) is a highly venomous snake native to coastal regions of East Africa. It is known for its bright green color and slender body, which helps it blend into trees. Despite its dangerous venom, it is generally shy and avoids human contact.

Malayan krait

The Malayan krait (Bungarus candidus) is a venomous snake found in Southeast Asia. It is easily recognized by its black and white banded pattern. This species is mostly active at night and possesses powerful neurotoxic venom that can affect the nervous system.

Mozambique spitting cobra

The Mozambique spitting cobra (Naja mossambica) is a venomous snake found across parts of Africa. It is known for its ability to spit venom accurately at threats, often aiming for the eyes. This species is defensive and plays an important role in controlling pests.

Inland taipan

The Inland taipan (Oxyuranus microlepidotus) is considered the most venomous snake in the world. It lives in remote regions of Australia and is rarely encountered. Although highly toxic, it is generally reclusive and avoids interaction with humans.

Saw-scaled viper

The saw-scaled viper (Echis carinatus) is a small but highly dangerous snake found in parts of Asia and Africa. It is known for its aggressive behavior and fast striking ability. This species is responsible for a large number of snakebite incidents in its habitat.

Mali cobra

The Mali cobra (Naja katiensis) is a venomous spitting cobra native to West Africa. It can project venom toward threats as a defense mechanism. This species is commonly found in savannas and plays a role in maintaining ecological balance.

Western brown snake

The Western brown snake (Pseudonaja nuchalis) is a highly venomous snake found in Australia. It is known for its adaptability to different environments and its potent venom. Despite its toxicity, it usually avoids humans unless provoked.

Caspian cobra

The Caspian cobra (Naja oxiana) is a venomous snake native to Central Asia. It is considered one of the most dangerous cobra species due to its strong neurotoxic venom. This species is typically found in dry and rocky habitats.
